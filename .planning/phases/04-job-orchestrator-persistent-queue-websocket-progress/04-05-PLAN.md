<objective>
Plan 04-05 closes CR-01 and CR-02 from 04-VERIFICATION.md: two restart-persistence holes in the boot interrupted-job sweep and the watchdog.

Purpose: CR-01 — `mark_interrupted_failed` (interrupt.py:78-82) and `run_watchdog` (queue.py:295-300) SELECT only `status IN ('ingesting','transcribing')`, excluding the transient `starting` status set by `pull_next`'s atomic claim (between `UPDATE jobs SET status='starting'` and run_job's first `update_stage("ingested")`). A crash in that window leaves the job permanently unrecoverable: pull_next skips it (not `queued`), the watchdog never marks it stale, the boot sweep never fails it, and `queue.cancel` cannot recover it after a restart because `_running` is empty. CR-02 — `mark_interrupted_failed` marks jobs failed based on status alone without consulting `infer_resume_point`. A job that crashed AFTER `update_stage("transcribed")` (DB status `transcribing`) but BEFORE `update_stage("done")` has a complete `transcript.json` on disk + `manifest.current_stage == "transcribed"`. On restart the sweep marks it `failed`, orphaning the user's completed transcription. `infer_resume_point` would have returned `"done"` (only the derived done transition remains), but the sweep never consults it.

Output: Two-line SELECT-filter widening (add `'starting'`) in BOTH `mark_interrupted_failed` and `run_watchdog` (CR-01); a per-job `infer_resume_point` consultation in `mark_interrupted_failed` that advances to `done` (via `update_stage`) when `resume_point is None or resume_point == "done"` INSTEAD of calling `mark_failed` (CR-02); two regression tests in `tests/test_orchestrator.py` (the home 04-02 used for restart-rejoin / boot-sweep tests). The filter-widening approach is chosen (NOT the alternative of having run_job call `update_stage("ingested")` first to make the `starting` window zero-width) because the latter would change 04-01's orchestrator wiring and conflict with 04-04.
</objective>
